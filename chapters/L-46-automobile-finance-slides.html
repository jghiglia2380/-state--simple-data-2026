Understanding Depreciation

9 / 20

New Cars Lose Value Fast

Depreciation is the silent cost of vehicle ownership. A new car loses value the moment you drive it off the lot, and the steepest decline happens in the first 2-3 years.

  • Year 1: Loses 20-30% of value (drive off lot = instant loss)
  • Year 2: Loses another 15-18% ($40K car ‚Üí ~$28K)
  • Year 3: Loses another 12-15% ($40K car ‚Üí ~$22K)
  • Year 5: Worth only ~40% of original ($40K ‚Üí ~$16K)
  • Years 6+: Depreciation slows significantly
  • Sweet Spot: 2-3 year old used car avoids steepest decline

üí° Strategy: Buy a 2-3 year old certified pre-owned vehicle and let someone else absorb the steepest depreciation.

Why Vehicle Decisions Matter

11 / 20

Second Largest Purchase of Your Life

For most people in {{STATE_NAME}}, vehicles are the second-largest financial decision after housing. The financing method you choose can result in tens of thousands of dollars in difference over your lifetime.

  • Monthly Cash Flow: High payments limit saving for emergencies and retirement
  • Opportunity Cost: $100K+ could have been invested instead
  • Credit Impact: Auto loans affect your credit score and debt-to-income
  • Financial Flexibility: Owning outright = no payment if income changes
  • Life Goals: Vehicle overspending delays home purchases, retirement
  • Compound Effect: Every $100/mo saved now = $120K over 30 years

üéØ The Goal: Transportation that meets your needs at the lowest total cost, freeing money for wealth-building.
