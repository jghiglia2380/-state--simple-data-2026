Charitable Giving Strategies

7 / 22

Ways to Give

  • Direct Cash Donations: Most common; provides immediate flexibility to charities
  • Appreciated Assets: Donate stocks/property; avoid capital gains tax while getting deduction
  • Donor-Advised Fund: "Charitable savings account"‚Äîimmediate deduction, distribute over time
  • Planned Giving: Include charity in will or estate plan for legacy giving
  • Recurring Giving: Monthly donations provide predictable funding for nonprofits
  • Matching Gifts: Many employers match charitable donations, doubling impact

üí° The best strategy depends on your income, assets, and charitable goals. Consider consulting a financial advisor for significant giving.

Key Metrics to Evaluate

10 / 22

üìä Financial Health

  • Program Expense Ratio: % spent on programs (aim for 75%+)
  • Fundraising Efficiency: Cost to raise $1 (under $0.25 is good)
  • Working Capital: Months of reserves (3-12 ideal)
  • Revenue Trends: Stable or growing funding

üîç Transparency & Governance

  • Board Independence: Diverse, independent board members
  • Executive Compensation: Reasonable for organization size
  • Financial Audits: Regular independent audits
  • Public Reporting: Annual reports, Form 990 available

üí° Context Matters: A 65% program ratio might be appropriate for a new or small charity building capacity. Look at trends over time, not just single-year snapshots.

Creating Your Giving Plan

15 / 22

Step 1: Set Your Budget

  • Determine what percentage of income to give
  • Common targets: 1%, 3%, 5%, or 10%
  • Factor in employer matching programs
  • Consider monthly vs. annual giving

Step 2: Identify Your Causes

  • What issues matter most to you?
  • Local vs. national vs. international impact?
  • Prevention vs. treatment approaches?
  • Direct service vs. advocacy vs. research?

Step 3: Research & Select

  • Use watchdog sites to evaluate options
  • Compare 3-5 charities in your cause area
  • Apply the PACED model
  • Focus on 2-3 organizations max

Step 4: Track & Adjust

  • Keep records for tax purposes
  • Review impact reports annually
  • Adjust giving as circumstances change
  • Celebrate your impact!
